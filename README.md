This Power BI project provides a macro-economic analysis of the Japanese labour market. Using national census data, the dashboard visualizes workforce concentration, regional economic profiles, and the gender dynamics defining the employment landscape.

## üîç Key Findings & Insights

### 1. The Labour Landscape
* **The Tokyo Magnet:** Tokyo is the undisputed economic hub with **7.6 million workers**.
* **The Clerical Economy:** Contrary to the image of an industrial nation, the leading profession in Japan is **Clerical** work, employing **14.5 million people**.
* **Employment Stability:** **61%** of Japan's total workforce are employed as "Regular Staff," providing a baseline of stability, though significant regional variations exist.

### 2. The Wealth Map
* **The Low-Income Reality:** A staggering **48%** of Japan's workforce falls into the "Low Income" bracket, highlighting a reliance on cost-efficient labour.
* **The Wealth Pathway:** High-income earners are strongly correlated with technical skills. **37%** of Japan's top-tier earners (Top Tier) are concentrated in a single sector: **Professional & Engineering**.

### 3. Workforce Dynamics
* **Gender Segregation in Administration:** Women hold a dominant share of the "Support Economy," making up **60%** of all Administrative roles.
* **The Income Gap:** The data visualizes a clear divergence in economic groups, with women disproportionately represented in lower-income and irregular employment tiers compared to their male counterparts.
